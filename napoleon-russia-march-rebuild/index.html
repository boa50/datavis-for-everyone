The road to Moscow

Different approaches to represent the data used by Charles Minard to illustrate Napoleon's catastrophic march to Moscow in 1812

Charles Minard's chart

Chart created by Charles Minard representing the shrinking size of Napoleon’s army as he marches from France all the way to Moscow, and then back home. The width of the line shows the number of French soldiers; the map shows their position as well as the date the army passed each location; other dimensions included in the visual are the temperatures during the winter portion of the army’s march, the geographic features (such as rivers) affecting travel, and the moments when the army split into multiple forces of varying sizes.
